DESTINATIONS

Thrissur

Often referred to as the Cultural Capital of Kerala, Thrissur is known for its grand festivals and heritage. The Thrissur Pooram festival, held at the Vadakkunnathan Temple, is one of the most spectacular temple festivals in India. The city also boasts attractions like the Athirappilly Waterfalls and the Guruvayur Temple.

Athirapally

Athirapilly Falls, located in Thrissur district, is Kerala's largest waterfall, standing tall at 81.5 feet. Nearby Vazhachal Falls is surrounded by dense forests, home to many endangered species.

Kalamandalam

Kerala Kalamandalam is a major center for learning Indian performing arts. Located on the banks of the Bharathapuzha river, it is a cultural institute dedicated to preserving traditional art forms.

Bekal

Bekal is a charming coastal town in Kasaragod district. The iconic Bekal Fort, overlooking the Arabian Sea, is a major attraction, offering breathtaking views and historical insights.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is rich in culture and history. It is famous for its beaches, historical sites, and delicious Malabar cuisine, including the world-famous Kozhikode biryani.
